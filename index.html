Ethical Hacking Process

Ethical hacking has become a knight in shining armour in the world of cybercrime. While cyberattacks remain rampant across organizations, industries, and the world, ethical hacking gives companies the solution to help them combat this ever-growing problem. There are different phases of ethical hacking.

[CRITICAL ALERT]: More often than not, it takes up to 6 months for organizations to realize that there has been a breach. A cyber attack has been said to occur every 39 seconds.

Organizations employ ethical hackers to simulate a real cyberattack on their systems and networks. This attack comes in different phases, used to pinpoint all areas of weaknesses that the organization faces to work towards strengthening them.

Phase 1: Reconnaissance

This is the first stage in the ethical hacking process. The white-hat hacker collects all the information available about the networks and systems in place, as well as the security measures that have been implemented.

Types of Reconnaissance:

  • Active reconnaissance: Looking for information about the target network system, server, or application. Increases the chance of detection.
  • Passive reconnaissance: Gaining information about key members, essential facts, IP addresses, and other critical information about the company. Stealthy.

Phase 2: Scanning Phase

This step involves using all the information obtained in the reconnaissance phase and applying it to look for vulnerabilities in the targeted area.

  • Scan for open ports or unprotected services.
  • Vulnerability scans to find weaknesses in company servers (often automated with TOOLS).
  • Network mapping: finding firewalls, routers, and networks.

Phase 3: Gaining Access

This is where the ethical hacker does the actual hacking. Using information from previous phases, a full-fledged attack is launched. The hacker exploits exposed vulnerabilities and gains control of the system.

  • Steal available data.
  • Corrupt systems.
  • Inject viruses or other malicious entities.

Phase 4: Maintaining Access

Usually, hackers have a mission to accomplish. Just breaking into the system is not enough. The hacker must maintain access to the server until the goal is fulfilled.

Key Techniques:

  • Trojans and other Backdoors.
  • Rootkits.
  • Launching further attacks from the compromised system.

Phase 5: Covering Tracks

This is the final step. A successful simulated attack means leaving the system without a trace. The sign of success is if the security system never realizes that an attack took place at all.

Measures Taken:

  • Deleting or Corrupting logs.
  • Modifying log values or registries.
  • Uninstalling applications and deleting folders created.
