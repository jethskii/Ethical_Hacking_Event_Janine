Breaking the OS Barrier

"Imagine your OS as a School Campus. Students are users, Teachers are admins, and Guards are the system protections. 'Breaking the Barrier' is entering the restricted records room without a key."
Case Study: The "Missing Quote" Logic Flaw

Attackers look for "Unquoted Service Paths"—essentially typos in the system's rulebook that allow unauthorized entry.

// How the system gets tricked:
Expected: "C:\Authorized Apps\Service.exe"
Broken: C:\Authorized Apps\Service.exe (No Quotes)
Result: The OS can be tricked into running a student's 'malicious' file instead of the teacher's 'service' file.
The Risk: When this barrier falls, the trust model is dead. An attacker becomes the teacher, rewriting the school rules at will.
