The Knight in Armour

Ethical hacking gives companies the solution to help them combat this ever-growing problem. In today's landscape, IT security conditions are dire.

"It takes up to 6 months for organizations to realize a breach has occurred. A cyber attack happens every 39 seconds."

Organizations employ ethical hackers to simulate a real cyberattack. This simulated attack is used to pinpoint all areas of weaknesses to work towards strengthening them.

01. Reconnaissance

The first stage where the white-hat hacker collects all information available about the networks, systems, and security measures.

  • Active Reconnaissance: Probing the target network system/server. High risk of detection.
  • Passive Reconnaissance: The stealthier way. Focusing on key members, IP addresses, and essential facts.
  • Gathering information about company's key members and public records.

02. Scanning Phase

Applying intelligence to look for vulnerabilities. This process is often automated with professional tools.

  • Vulnerability Scans: Finding weaknesses in company servers.
  • Network Mapping: Identifying firewalls, routers, and host networks.
  • Open Port Analysis: Scanning for services running unprotected.
# nmap -sV -p 1-65535 target_ip
# scanning_vulnerabilities... DONE

03. Gaining Access

The hacker exploits exposed vulnerabilities and gains control of the system.

  • Launch full-fledged attack based on Phase 1 & 2 data.
  • Steal data, corrupt systems, or inject viruses.
  • Manipulate network entities to gain deeper control.

04. Maintaining Access

Breaking in isn't enough; the ethical hacker must maintain access until the goal is fulfilled.

  • Persistence: Utilizing Trojans, Backdoors, or Rootkits.
  • Launching secondary attacks from the internal beachhead.
  • Ensuring the connection survives reboots and patches.

05. Covering Tracks

The sign of a successful simulated attack is that the security system never realizes it occurred.

  • Log Manipulation: Deleting or corrupting system logs.
  • Registry Modification: Altering values to hide persistence.
  • Total Cleanup: Deleting folders and uninstalling all hacking tools used.
  • Removing all traces of any activity performed.
# history -c
# rm -rf /tmp/payload
# logs_cleared: TRUE
