Knight in Armour

In the digital age, Ethical Hacking is the frontline defense. As cyberattacks become more rampant, we provide the solution to combat this ever-growing global problem.

"A cyber attack occurs every 39 seconds. Age-old systems are not equipped to handle them. We are here to simulate, identify, and strengthen."
  • Critical Window: Organizations take up to 6 months to realize a breach has occurred.
  • Continuous Upgrades: Regular updates to IT security are the only way to safeguard data.
  • The Goal: Identify all vulnerabilities and exploit them to pinpoint areas of weakness.

Reconnaissance

The collection phase. We gather everything available about the network and implemented security measures.

  • Passive Recon: Stealthy gathering of IP addresses, employee data, and company facts.
  • Active Recon: Directly probing the system. Riskier, but yields higher quality technical data.
  • The OSINT Advantage: Using public information to map the attack surface easily.

Scanning & Mapping

Applying intelligence to find the specific "open windows" in the target architecture.

  • Port Scanning: Finding unprotected services and open ports.
  • Vulnerability Scans: Automated tools identifying weaknesses in company servers.
  • Network Mapping: Locating firewalls, routers, and host networks for the strategy.

Gaining Access

The "Point of Infiltration." We launch a full-fledged attack on the identified exposed vulnerabilities.

  • Exploitation: Gaining full control of the system or network.
  • Action on Objectives: Stealing data, corrupting systems, or injecting viruses.
  • Malicious Entities: Manipulating the environment to the hacker's benefit.

Persistence

Ensuring the connection remains stable even after reboots or security sweeps.

  • Trojans & Backdoors: Maintaining a secret "side door" into the server.
  • Rootkits: Hiding the hacker's presence at the OS kernel level.
  • Ongoing Damage: Using the access to launch secondary attacks deeper into the organization.

Track Obfuscation

Leaving the system without a trace. If successful, the security system never realizes an attack occurred.

  • Log Manipulation: Deleting or corrupting system logs and registry values.
  • Resource Cleanup: Deleting folders and uninstalling all applications used.
  • Total Erasure: Removing all traces of activity to avoid detection.
