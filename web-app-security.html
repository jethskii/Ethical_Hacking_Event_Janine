Web App Security

1. Broken Access Control

IS Focus: Identity & Access Management (IAM)

The failure to restrict users from accessing data or functions outside their intended permissions. In IS, this leads to unauthorized data disclosure and loss of privacy.

Q: If a user changes the URL to see another person's info, what is violated?
A: The Principle of Least Privilege.

2. SQL Injection (SQLi)

IS Focus: Data Integrity & Database Governance

An attacker "injects" malicious SQL code into web forms to manipulate the backend database. This can destroy inventory records or leak financial data.

Q: Why is SQLi dangerous for business reporting?
A: It allows hackers to delete or alter records, making the Information System unreliable.

3. Cryptographic Failures

IS Focus: Compliance & Data Privacy Acts

Using weak encryption or no encryption at all. In 2026, failing to protect sensitive PII (Personally Identifiable Information) results in massive legal fines.

Q: Is "Password123" safe if the site is HTTPS?
A: No. HTTPS protects data in transit, but Cryptographic Failure refers to data stored on the server.

4. Insecure Design

IS Focus: SDLC (System Development Life Cycle)

Flaws in the system architecture. This isn't a coding bug, but a design error that allows for exploitation (e.g., lack of automated logic checks).

Ice Breaker: Can a perfectly coded app still be hacked?
Answer: Yes, if the "Plan" (Design) was flawed from the start.

5. Software & Data Integrity

IS Focus: Supply Chain Risk Management

Web apps rely on external libraries (NPM/APIs). If an automatic update includes malicious code, the entire Information System is compromised.

Q: What is an SBOM?
A: Software Bill of Materials—a list of every "ingredient" in your software.

6. Social Engineering (Deepfakes)

IS Focus: Security Awareness & Training

Manipulating employees into giving up web credentials. In 2026, AI-generated voice and video are used to trick IT admins into resetting passwords.

Q: What is the "Human Firewall"?
A: Employees who are trained to spot and report phishing attempts.

7. Business Continuity (BCP)

IS Focus: Availability & Disaster Recovery

Security isn't just about blocking hackers; it's about keeping the system online. DDoS attacks can shut down a web app, stopping all business operations.

Final Q: What is more important: Confidentiality or Availability?
A: In Information Systems, it depends on the business needs (The CIA Triad).
